Chicken Mandi

Chicken Mandi is a traditional Yemeni rice and chicken dish known for its smoky aroma and subtle spices. The chicken is slow-cooked until tender and served over fragrant long-grain rice infused with whole spices. It’s light, flavourful, and perfect for a comforting meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste
  • 2 dried limes (loomi) or bay leaves
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required

Directions

  1. Prepare the chicken
    Clean and season the chicken with salt, turmeric, cumin, and coriander powder. Set aside.
  2. Cook the base
    Heat oil in a large pot and sauté onions until soft. Add garlic and ginger paste and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add spices and chicken
    Add whole spices and chicken pieces. Cook on medium heat for 5–7 minutes until lightly browned.
  4. Cook the rice
    Add washed rice, water, and dried limes. Cover and cook on low heat until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy.
  5. Serve
    Serve hot with chicken placed over the rice. Enjoy with mandi sauce or yogurt.
